What is Foodscience

Food Science

Food science is a multidisciplinary field that applies scientific principles to the study, understanding, and improvement of food. It encompasses various scientific disciplines, including biology, chemistry, physics, engineering, and nutrition. The primary goal of food science is to ensure the production of safe, nutritious, and appealing food products.

Goal 2 : Zero hunger

As of 2020, approximately 811 million people globally suffered from hunger. Malnutrition affected 690 million individuals, while one-third of food produced (1.3 billion tons) was lost or wasted annually. Food science addresses these challenges through nutrient enhancement, sustainable agriculture, and food preservation, contributing to the goal of zero hunger

Goal 2 : Solution

To address the challenge of hunger, the implementation of smart crop monitoring technology emerges as a crucial solution. This innovative approach utilizes real-time data from sensors and satellite imagery to monitor crop health, optimize resource allocation, and detect potential diseases early on. By introducing precision farming techniques, farmers gain valuable insights for informed decision-making, maximizing yields and promoting sustainable agricultural practices. Smart crop monitoring not only enhances efficiency but also contributes to building a resilient and abundant food supply, playing a pivotal role in the ongoing efforts to combat hunger and food insecurity.

SCMT

Smart crop monitoring technology (SCMT) uses sensors and analytics for real-time data on crop health and environmental factors. These sensors measure soil moisture, temperature, and humidity. Analyzing this data offers insights into crop growth, empowering farmers to make informed decisions. Optimization of irrigation and fertilization enhances efficiency and reduces resource usage. Aligned with the UN's zero hunger goal, this technology boosts agricultural productivity for a sustainable food supply chain. It's a crucial tool in addressing global food security challenges.
